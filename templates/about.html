Smart Ethereum Transactions

The gas mechanism on the Ethereum blockchain attempts to set charges for every smart contract operation in order to prevent infinite control of computational resources by executing transactions. To satisfy this requirement, users need to specify in their transactions how much gas fees (in terms of gas limit and gas price) they would like to pay for. In essence, these gas fees are paid to the miners in return for their computational services. Naturally, miners tend to maximize their profits by considering the transactions with higher gas fees, and as a result, the transactions with lower gas fees remain in the waiting pool for a long time. This web app is a scaled down version of a machine learning model based for prediction of transaction waiting time on ethereum blockchain based on transaction parameters. This a scaled down version, for the complete theoritical work please refer to the research paper.
